THEORY NOTES

STRESS
stress = force/unit area
describes how material resists deformation

stress tensor (for 3D solid):
     Fx  Fy  Fz
x  [ xx  xy  xz ]
y  [ yx  yy  yz ]
z  [ zx  zy  zz ]

diagonal entries: normal stresses
others are shear stresses

b/c stress tensor is symmetric (xy = yx, etc.),
Voigt notation lists [xx, yy, zz, yz, xz, xy]

bulk stress tensor: tensor for unit cell, describes overall mechanical state of material
per-atom stress: separate tensor for each atom, decomposes bulk stress into atomic contributions
                 finds where stress is localized (ie. near a defect or surface)


FUNCTION MINIMIZATION
Newton's method: use gradient 'g' (1st deriv.) and Hessian 'H' (2nd deriv.) matrix to find min w/
    x_new = x_old - H⁻¹ · g
    (Hessian gives curvature, so you take more well-informed steps than w/ gradient descent)

computing 3N x 3N derivs for Hessian is expensive, so BFGS approximates H and updates with each step
