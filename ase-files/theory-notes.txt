THEORY NOTES

STRESS
stress = force/unit area
describes how material resists deformation

stress tensor (for 3D solid):
     Fx  Fy  Fz
x  [ xx  xy  xz ]
y  [ yx  yy  yz ]
z  [ zx  zy  zz ]

diagonal entries: normal stresses
others are shear stresses

b/c stress tensor is symmetric (xy = yx, etc.),
Voigt notation lists [xx, yy, zz, yz, xz, xy]

bulk stress tensor: tensor for unit cell, describes overall mechanical state of material
per-atom stress: separate tensor for each atom, decomposes bulk stress into atomic contributions
                 finds where stress is localized (ie. near a defect or surface)


FUNCTION MINIMIZATION
Newton's method: use gradient 'g' (1st deriv.) and Hessian 'H' (2nd deriv.) matrix to find min w/
    x_new = x_old - H⁻¹ · g
    (Hessian gives curvature, so you take more well-informed steps than w/ gradient descent)

computing 3N x 3N derivs for Hessian is expensive, so BFGS approximates H and updates with each step


ADSORPTION
absorption: substance permeates interior (bulk) of material
adsorption: substance adheres to surface of material
    physisorption: weak van der Waals interaction, molecule barely disturbed, easily reversible
                   ex. water condensation
    chemisorption: actual chemical bond forms between surface and material, stronger
                   ex. oxygen bonds with iron's surface --> iron oxide

adsorption energy: energy change when molecule binds to surface
                   E_ads = E(slab + adsorbate) - E(slab) - E(molecule)
                   negative ==> favorable
    impacted by: 
        • d-band of metal rel. to Fermi level
        • molecule's orbitals available to interact
        • geometry (site, surface facet)

homogeneous catalysis: catalyst and reactants in same phase (mixed in solution together)
heterogeneous catalysis: catalyst and reactants are in different phases
    ex. Haber-Bosch process. N₂ + H₂ → NH₃ over an iron surface (gasses flow over it)
